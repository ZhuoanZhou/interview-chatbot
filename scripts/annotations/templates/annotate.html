Task 2: Rate Emergent Insights

{{ session_data.emergent_items|length }} emergent insights to rate

Rating Guidelines (1-5 scale for each dimension):

Topical Relevance:

How much did this emergent subtopic add new dimensions within the planned topic being discussed?

  • 1 - Irrelevant or distracting
  • 2 - Weakly relevant with limited connection
  • 3 - Somewhat relevant but not fully aligned
  • 4 - Mostly relevant and clearly connected
  • 5 - Highly relevant and directly aligned with the subtopic
Topic-Level Emergence:

How much did this discussion reveal novel or unexpected perspectives within this topic area? Did it go beyond obvious aspects to uncover new angles?

💡 Example: Rate high if you'd want to ask other interviewees about this emergent direction because it opens important discussions. Rate low if the discussion stayed within predictable boundaries of the topic.

  • 1 - Adds nothing new beyond the subtopic; not emergent
  • 2 - Adds minor new connections but largely redundant
  • 3 - Moderately emergent with some novel insight
  • 4 - Clearly emergent and adds meaningful new perspectives
  • 5 - Highly emergent, introducing substantial novel directions
Surprise:

How unexpected were the emergent ideas or answers relative to what had already been discussed?

  • 1 - Entirely expected or routine
  • 2 - Slightly unexpected
  • 3 - Moderately unexpected
  • 4 - Highly unexpected
  • 5 - Strongly surprising while remaining relevant
Analytical Value:

How much does this emergent insight contribute to understanding AI workforce issues across the industry? What's the overall utility for research?

💡 Example: Rate high if this provides valuable insights for understanding AI use/impact across roles and industries. Rate low if it's interesting for this specific person but has limited broader analytical value.

  • 1 - Distracting or uninformative
  • 2 - Adds little analytical insight
  • 3 - Some insight but limited evaluative usefulness
  • 4 - Adds meaningful analytical insight
  • 5 - Substantially deepens understanding or evaluative power
